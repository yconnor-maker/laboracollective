Why Congress

Every major advance in maternal health came through Congress — not the executive, not the courts:

Medicaid (1965) — 42% of all births
Title X (1970) — low-income repro care
WIC (1972) — mothers & children nutrition
EMTALA (1986) — emergency guarantee
FMLA (1993) — job protection
CHIP (1997) — 10M children covered
ACA (2010) — maternity essential benefit
Preventing Maternal Deaths (2018)
PUMP Act (2022) — breastfeeding
Pregnant Workers Act (2023)
The health of women and children is directly correlated to the success of a nation. This is not a women's issue. This is critical national infrastructure. A nation cannot prosper, survive, or thrive without it.
