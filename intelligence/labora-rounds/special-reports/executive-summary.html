Executive Summary

The State of Women's & Children's Health in America • March 2026

The U.S. obstetrical care system was a car that was already breaking down — old, neglected, running on deferred maintenance. Then someone forgot the oil change. Then the transmission blew. And now, on the side of the road with a busted transmission, the current administration is stripping the car for parts.

This summary traces the arc of decline across four phases: the first Trump term, the COVID pandemic, the Dobbs decision, and the first year of the second Trump term. The data shows how each phase compounded the last — and how we arrived at the most dangerous moment for women's and children's health in modern American history.

The Case

By the Numbers: The Facts That Prove the Thesis

In Texas, white women's maternal mortality increased 95% after the abortion ban.

Texas maternal mortality surged 56% overall from 2019–2022 — five times the national rate. The racial breakdown flips every assumption: white women +95%, Black women +38%, Hispanic women +30%. The single largest increase was among white women — the policy itself is driving the deaths. Texas maternal hospital deaths: 79 → 120. The state has refused to review maternal deaths from 2022–2023.

The policy designed to force birth is producing fewer births.

Texas enacted the most restrictive ban in the country. Texas's fertility rate dropped to a new state low. Vasectomies among young men surged 95% after Dobbs. Tubal sterilizations among women 19–26 leapt 70%. The U.S. fertility rate hit an all-time low of under 1.6 — far below the 2.1 replacement rate. The pro-natalist agenda produced an anti-natalist outcome.

264 women were turned in to law enforcement by their own doctors.

In two years post-Dobbs, 412 criminal charges for pregnancy outcomes — an 8× increase. Alabama: 192. Oklahoma: 112. 75% low-income. 264 from medical disclosure. Women are avoiding prenatal care — the single most effective intervention — by 40–60% in border regions because seeing a doctor can result in criminal charges.

The #1 cause of death for pregnant women is homicide.

Not hemorrhage. Not hypertension. Not infection. Homicide — at 3.62 per 100,000, exceeding every obstetric cause combined by 2×. 68% involve firearms. After Dobbs, DV increased 7–10% in ban states. Each additional restriction → +3.4% intimate partner homicide rate. When a woman cannot end a pregnancy, she cannot leave the man who is killing her.

937% increase in a disease prevented by a $30 shot — and the shot is in shortage.

Congenital syphilis: 335 cases (2012) → 4,000 (2024). 282 babies dead or disabled in 2022 alone. Every death preventable with one penicillin injection. The drug has been in shortage since 2023. The CDC team tracking the epidemic was fired. The government's response to a 937% increase was to eliminate the people tracking it.

Black women who earn $100,000+ still die at higher rates than white women in poverty.

The racial disparity is not explained by income. The system is the variable. Black women die at 3.5× the white rate. The response: target the only NIH institute studying why ($223.5M in grants terminated), destroy the only surveillance system measuring it (PRAMS fired), cut Medicaid — which covers 64% of births to Black mothers.

Fentanyl-xylazine deaths: 99 in 2018 → 6,020 in 2023.

A 6,000% increase. Overdose is the #1 medical cause of maternal death. Treatment infrastructure: $2 billion in SAMHSA grants terminated overnight. Fewer than 500 reproductive psychiatrists for 3.6 million births/year. 75% of perinatal mood disorders go untreated. The wait: 48 days. For a woman on fentanyl, 48 days is a death sentence.

The "pro-family" movement made IVF legally dangerous.

Alabama Supreme Court: frozen embryos are "children." Three clinics shut down. Personhood language in 11 state laws. Republicans blocked the Right to IVF Act. 1 in 8 couples need IVF. The movement that claims to champion families made the most effective family-building technology a legal liability.

Women are deleting their health apps because their data can prosecute them.

50 million Americans use period-tracking apps. After HIPAA reproductive privacy was vacated, 30% of users in ban states deleted them. Pharmacy records, search history, cell phone location: now available for prosecution. Women are disconnecting from digital health — the primary interface for prenatal care — because their data has become evidence.

Phase One: 2017–2020

He Lit the Fuse

Three Supreme Court appointments — Gorsuch, Kavanaugh, Barrett — created the majority that would overturn Roe v. Wade. This is the single most consequential action of the first term for women's health.

The fuse was lit. The detonation would come two years later.

The Path Forward

Why Congress

Every major advance in maternal and child health in American history came through Congress:

  • Medicaid (1965) — funds 42% of all births
  • Title X (1970) — low-income reproductive care
  • WIC (1972) — nutrition for mothers & children
  • EMTALA (1986) — emergency care guarantee
  • FMLA (1993) — job protection for new mothers
  • CHIP (1997) — 10 million children covered
  • ACA (2010) — maternity as essential benefit
  • Preventing Maternal Deaths Act (2018)
  • PUMP Act (2022) — breastfeeding protections
  • Pregnant Workers Fairness Act (2023)

Every single one came from Congress. Not the executive branch. Not the courts. From Congress. And right now, only Congress can rebuild what has been dismantled.

Every person reading this either is a woman, knows a woman, has a woman in their family, or is married to a woman.

But beyond the obvious — the health of women and children, and particularly infant mortality, is directly correlated to the success and prospects of a nation. This is not a women's issue. This is critical national infrastructure. The obstetrical care system is critical infrastructure, the same way bridges and power grids are critical infrastructure.

A nation cannot prosper, survive, or thrive without this infrastructure in place. Every single person should care about this — because if you care about the success of this nation, you have to care about this issue.
