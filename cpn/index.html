Clinical Partners Network

A physician-led partnership for women's health

The Clinical Partners Network (CPN) is Labora Collective's collaboration architecture — an invitation-only partnership model, an open-membership pathway, and a marketplace that connects clinicians, doulas, organizations, and institutions working in women's health.

Master Architecture · Reconciled V2
1 — Invited Partnership

The five-tier collaboration structure

The network is anchored by an invitation-only partnership model with five distinct levels of collaboration. Invited partners receive platform access at no cost in exchange for their clinical contributions and distribution capacity.

Tier 1

Clinical Advisor

Credentialed clinicians and researchers. Review one Labora Rounds report per quarter for clinical accuracy. Receive early access and full LC membership.

Tier 2

Clinical Contributor

Clinicians, birth workers, and patients. Submit written pieces or voice notes for publication in LC journals with editorial support and bylined publication.

Tier 3

Clinical Partner

Organizations with their own audiences — e.g., doula collectives. Distribute LC content and co-host events. Receive five institutional seats and content licensing rights.

Tier 4

Strategic Partner

Major institutions and foundations. Co-develop research and clinical frameworks. Receive co-ownership credit and custom Labora Rounds intelligence.

Tier 5

Institutional Partner

Hospital networks and large training programs. License LC content for deployment across their systems at scale, under enterprise licensing terms.

2 — Open Membership

The paid access pathway

For non-invited professionals who wish to join the CPN ecosystem, there is a paid pathway with three access tiers — built for individual clinicians, advanced practitioners, and small collectives.

CPN Member

$97 / year

Full vault access, marketplace listing, and early event invitations.

CPN Pro

$197 / year

Adds Track 1 publication priority, clinical briefings, and inclusion in the Doula's Corner template ecosystem.

CPN Organization

$497 / year

Designed for small collectives. Includes five shared seats and co-branding permissions for client handouts.

3 — Co-Created Products

The 60 / 40 revenue split

Educational modules are standalone products. Membership grants platform access and visibility; educational products — CE modules, masterclasses, templates — are purchased separately by the audience.

The revenue share

When an invited partner co-authors an educational product with Labora Collective, revenue is split 60% to the creator — who provides the clinical expertise — and 40% to LC, who provides the instructional design, platform, and marketing.

4 — The Clinical Marketplace

Frictionless booking, aligned incentives

The Marketplace is a booking ecosystem for clients seeking verified doulas, lactation consultants, and holistic care providers. It launches concurrently with the inaugural cohort to establish network density from day one.

  • No listing fees. Providers are never charged simply to be listed in the directory — visibility is included with membership.
  • 10% booking commission. LC takes a flat 10% on any services booked through the platform. This covers the ~2.9% Stripe processing fee and leaves ~7% as net margin to maintain the infrastructure.
  • Incentive alignment. By avoiding extractive listing fees, LC only earns when the practitioner actually secures a client.
5 — The Intelligence Engine

One piece of intelligence per quarter

To power the Queryable Clinical LLM and sustain the network's clinical value, invited partners submit one piece of clinical intelligence per quarter to retain free status. Submissions arrive via one of two tracks.

Track 1

The Column

A written case study, evidence-based protocol, or clinical deep-dive, published under the partner's byline.

Track 2

The Story

A dictated audio or video interview capturing frontline observations, synthesized into publishable content by the LC editorial team.
