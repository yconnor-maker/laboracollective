Definitive Program Governance (Reconciled V2)

1. The 5-Tier Collaboration Structure

The network is anchored by an invitation-only partnership model with 5 distinct levels of collaboration. Invited partners receive platform access for free in exchange for their contributions and distribution capacity.

Tier 1: Clinical Advisor

Credentialed clinicians and researchers. They review 1 Labora Rounds report per quarter for clinical accuracy and receive early access and full LC membership.

Tier 2: Clinical Contributor

Clinicians, birth workers, and patients. They submit written pieces or voice notes for publication in LC journals, receiving editorial support and bylined publication.

Tier 3: Clinical Partner

Organizations with their own audiences (e.g., doula collectives). They distribute LC content and co-host events, receiving 5 institutional seats and content licensing rights.

Tier 4: Strategic Partner

Major institutions and foundations. They co-develop research and clinical frameworks, receiving co-ownership credit and custom Labora Rounds intelligence.

Tier 5: Institutional Partner

Hospital networks and large training programs. They license LC content for deployment across their systems at scale, paying enterprise licensing fees.

2. The Open Membership Pathway (Paid Access)

For non-invited professionals who wish to access the CPN network, there is a paid pathway with three access tiers:

CPN Member

$97 / Year
Includes full vault access, marketplace listing, and early event invitations.

CPN Pro

$197 / Year
Adds Track 1 publication priority, clinical briefings, and inclusion in the Doula's Corner template ecosystem.

CPN Organization

$497 / Year
The middle-ground tier for small collectives. Includes 5 shared seats and co-branding permissions for client handouts.

2. Co-Created Products (The 60/40 Split)

Educational Modules are Standalone Products. They are not given away for free inside the $97/mo membership. The membership grants platform access and visibility; the educational products (CE Modules, Masterclasses, Templates) are purchased separately by the audience.

The Revenue Share: When an Invited Partner co-authors an educational product with LC, the revenue is split 60% to the Creator (who provides the clinical expertise) and 40% to LC (who provides the instructional design, platform, and marketing).

3. The Clinical Marketplace

The Marketplace is a frictionless booking ecosystem for clients seeking verified doulas, lactation consultants, and holistic care providers. It will launch concurrently to the entire inaugural cohort to immediately establish network density.

  • No Listing Fees: Providers are never charged simply to be listed in the directory. Visibility is included in their membership.
  • 10% Booking Commission: LC takes a flat 10% commission on any services booked through the platform. (This covers the ~2.9% Stripe processing fee and leaves ~7% as net margin to maintain the infrastructure).
  • Incentive Alignment: By avoiding extractive listing fees, LC only makes money when the practitioner actually secures a client.

4. The Intelligence Engine (The Quota)

To power the Queryable Clinical LLM and maintain the high value of the network, Invited Partners are required to submit one (1) piece of clinical intelligence per quarter to retain their free status. This intelligence is submitted via:

  • Track 1 (Column): A written case study, evidence-based protocol, or clinical deep-dive.
  • Track 2 (Story): A dictated audio/video interview capturing frontline observations, synthesized into content by the LC editorial team.
