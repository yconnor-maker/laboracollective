Labora Collective

About Labora Collective

Physician-led intelligence for women's health — clinical, political, economic, and policy reporting for practitioners, advocates, and the women they serve.

Our Mission

Rigorous enough for a department chair.
Legible enough for a campaign staffer.
Urgent enough for a patient.

The clinical evidence on women's health moves. Policy changes overnight. Economic forces reshape what care is available before a single guideline is updated. Labora Collective exists to close that gap — physician-authored intelligence that connects what's happening in courts, legislatures, funding agencies, and research trials to what it means in the exam room on Monday morning.

This is not advocacy. It is impact documentation — the measurable clinical, economic, and legal consequences of the decisions being made about women's health right now.

We publish three times per week across three journals covering the full spectrum of women's health, from clinical care and reproductive justice to policy analysis and infrastructure reporting. Our intelligence products — The Labora Signal, The Labora Briefing, and Labora Rounds — deliver original, physician-authored reporting to clinicians, researchers, advocates, doulas, and the women they serve.

Founder
Dr. Yamicia D. Connor
MD PhD MPH · Founder, Diosa Ara · Publisher, Labora Collective

Dr. Connor is a practicing OB-GYN who has watched brilliant clinical evidence fail to reach patients — not because providers don't care, but because no one built the system to close the distance between what science knows and what policy allows.

Labora Collective is the intelligence publishing arm of Diosa Ara, an obstetrical emergency infrastructure organization advancing clinical decision support, escalation architecture, and evidence-based advocacy for women's health.

What We Cover

Three journals. Eleven intelligence domains. One complete picture.

Women's Health Journal

Clinical education, physiological health, and empowered care — five sub-journals covering GYN, OB, personalized medicine, sexual health, and lifecycle wellness.

In Her Name

Political, investigative, and systems reporting — six sub-journals documenting legislative impact, racial bias, economic violence, and reproductive rights.

The Blueprint

Research, implementation, and infrastructure — Labora Rounds, The Birthwork Brief, and practitioner intelligence for doulas, midwives, and providers.
